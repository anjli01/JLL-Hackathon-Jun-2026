# Climate Risk Insurance — Commercial Real Estate Strategy

## Overview
Climate-related risks are fundamentally reshaping the commercial real estate insurance market. Properties exposed to flood, wildfire, hurricanes, and severe weather face rapidly escalating premiums, higher deductibles, and in some cases, inability to obtain coverage. For CRE investors and brokers, insurance costs and availability are becoming critical factors in property valuation, acquisition due diligence, and asset management.

## Insurance Market Dynamics (2025–2026)

### Premium Trends
| Risk Profile | Premium Trend | Notes |
|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Low-risk, well-maintained | Stable to -5-15% | Carrier competition increasing |
| Moderate climate exposure | Flat to +5% | Market stabilizing for diversified risks |
| High flood risk (Zone AE/VE) | +15–40% annually | NFIP Risk Rating 2.0 driving increases |
| High wildfire risk | +20–50% annually | California, Colorado, Pacific NW most affected |
| Coastal hurricane exposure | +10–30% annually | Florida, Gulf Coast, Southeast |
| Non-mitigated/non-compliant | +30–100% or non-renewal | Carriers refusing unmitigated risks |

### Key Drivers
- **Increased catastrophic losses**: Global insured losses exceeded $145 billion in 2025 — driven by "secondary perils" (wildfires, floods, convective storms).
- **Reinsurance costs**: Reinsurers have raised attachment points and rates, passing costs to primary carriers and ultimately property owners.
- **Advanced risk modeling**: Carriers now use granular, property-specific risk models (RMS, CoreLogic, Moody's) instead of broad geographic zones — creating wider premium spreads between mitigated and unmitigated properties.
- **Regulatory and legal changes**: Climate litigation, state insurance regulations, and building code requirements affecting coverage terms.

## NFIP Risk Rating 2.0
The National Flood Insurance Program's Risk Rating 2.0 (effective 2021–2023 phased rollout) fundamentally changed how flood premiums are calculated:

### How It Works
- **Individual property rating**: Instead of flood zone-based pricing, each property is rated based on specific risk factors:
  - Distance to water source and type of water body
  - First floor elevation relative to Base Flood Elevation (BFE)
  - Building characteristics (foundation type, number of floors)
  - Historical flood frequency at the property location
  - Cost to rebuild (replacement value)
- **Impact**: Some properties see premium decreases, but many properties in high-risk areas face dramatic increases (up to 18% per year, capped by law).

### Mitigation Credits
Properties that implement flood mitigation measures can receive premium credits:
| Mitigation Measure | Potential Premium Reduction |
|--------------------|---------------------------|
| Elevated first floor above BFE | 20–60% reduction |
| Flood openings (vents) | 5–15% reduction |
| Machine elevating (HVAC, electrical) | 10–20% reduction |
| Flood barriers/walls | 10–25% reduction |
| Backflow prevention | 5–10% reduction |

## Wildfire Insurance Market
The wildfire insurance market has experienced the most dramatic disruption:

### California
- **FAIR Plan**: State-run insurer of last resort. FAIR Plan policies have surged as major carriers (State Farm, Allstate, AIG) have reduced or exited California wildfire exposure.
- **Moratorium on non-renewal**: California DOI has periodically imposed moratoriums preventing carriers from non-renewing policies in wildfire-affected areas.
- **Wildfire mitigation requirements**: Carriers increasingly require documented defensible space, ember-resistant roofing, and vegetation management as conditions of coverage.

### Premium Impact
- **WUI (Wildland-Urban Interface) properties**: 25–100% premium increases over 2020–2025 period.
- **Properties with documented mitigation**: 15–30% lower premiums than unmitigated peers.
- **IBHS Fortified designation**: Properties meeting IBHS Fortified standards receive preferential pricing from participating carriers.

## How Mitigation Reduces Insurance Costs
Documented physical resilience measures directly translate to insurance savings:

| Mitigation Category | Insurance Benefit | Typical Savings |
|--------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Flood barriers & elevated mechanicals** | NFIP premium reduction, lower private flood rates | 20–50% of flood premium |
| **Ember-resistant roof & defensible space** | Wildfire coverage availability, reduced premiums | 15–30% of property premium |
| **Hurricane clips & impact-resistant windows** | Wind/hail premium reduction | 10–25% of wind premium |
| **Fire sprinkler systems** | Property premium reduction | 5–15% of total premium |
| **Seismic retrofit** | Earthquake premium reduction | 10–30% of earthquake premium |
| **Backup power (generator/BESS)** | Business interruption coverage terms | 5–10% of BI premium |

## Parametric Insurance
An emerging insurance product relevant to climate-exposed CRE:
- **How it works**: Pays a pre-agreed amount when a defined trigger occurs (e.g., wind speed > 130 mph, rainfall > 6 inches in 24 hours), regardless of actual damage.
- **Advantages**: No claims adjustment process — payment within days. Fills gaps left by traditional policies.
- **Use cases**: Business interruption coverage, deductible buy-downs, climate risk layers that traditional carriers won't cover.
- **Availability**: Growing market — available through specialty carriers like Swiss Re, Munich Re, and parametric-focused startups.

## Insurance as a Stranding Risk Indicator
Insurance availability and cost increasingly serve as early warning signals for asset stranding:
- **Non-renewable properties**: Properties that cannot obtain insurance at reasonable cost face financing challenges (lenders require insurance).
- **Valuation impact**: Rising insurance costs directly reduce NOI (Net Operating Income) and therefore property valuation.
- **Due diligence**: Institutional investors now include insurance market analysis in acquisition due diligence.
- **Lender requirements**: CMBS and commercial lenders may require specific coverage levels; inability to obtain coverage can trigger loan default provisions.

## Key Metrics for Assessment
- Current insurance premiums as % of property value
- Premium trend over past 3–5 years
- Deductible levels (especially % deductibles for wind, earthquake, flood)
- NFIP Risk Rating 2.0 score and premium trajectory
- Distance to nearest fire station, water source, WUI boundary
- Documented mitigation measures and their impact on premiums
- Availability of coverage (number of carrier quotes received)
- Business interruption coverage adequacy vs. actual exposure
