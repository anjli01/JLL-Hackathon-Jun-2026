# Wildfire Resilience — Commercial Building Protection

## Overview
Wildfire risk is increasing across the Western U.S., with the wildland-urban interface (WUI) expanding as development pushes into fire-prone areas. Commercial properties in or near WUI zones face significant physical and financial risk from wildfires.

## Mitigation Measures

### Building Hardening
1. **Ember-resistant roofing** ($8–15/sq ft re-roof)
   - Class A fire-rated materials (concrete tile, metal, clay)
   - Eliminate wood shakes and shingles
   - Ember-resistant vents with 1/8" mesh screens

2. **Non-combustible siding** ($5–12/sq ft)
   - Fiber cement, stucco, brick, stone
   - Replace vinyl and wood siding within 0–5 feet of ground

3. **Dual-pane tempered glass** ($15–30/sq ft window area)
   - Resists radiant heat exposure
   - Prevents window failure and interior ignition

4. **Fire-resistant decking and attachments** ($10–20/sq ft)
   - Composite or metal decking materials
   - Non-combustible fencing within 5 feet of structure

### Defensible Space
1. **Zone 0 (0–5 feet)**: Non-combustible zone — gravel, hardscape, no vegetation
2. **Zone 1 (5–30 feet)**: Lean, clean, green — irrigated, low-height plants, no ladder fuels
3. **Zone 2 (30–100 feet)**: Reduced fuel zone — spacing between trees, mowed grass
4. **Zone 3 (100–200 feet)**: Extended defense — fire breaks, managed vegetation

## Insurance Considerations
- **Wildfire-specific policies**: Separate wildfire coverage increasingly required in high-risk zones
- **Firewise USA certification**: Community-level mitigation program that can reduce premiums
- **California FAIR Plan**: Insurer of last resort for high-risk properties (limited coverage)
- **Mitigation credits**: Many insurers offer 5–15% premium reduction for documented hardening measures
