# Heat Pump Technology — Commercial Applications

## Overview
Heat pumps are the primary electrification technology for commercial building decarbonization. They transfer heat rather than generating it through combustion, achieving 200–400% efficiency (COP 2–4) compared to gas boilers at 80–95%.

## Types for Commercial Buildings

### Air-Source Heat Pumps (ASHP)
- **COP range**: 2.5–4.0 (depending on outdoor temperature)
- **Cold-climate models (ccASHP)**: Operate efficiently down to -15°F with COP ≥ 2.0
- **Cost**: $15–25 per square foot of conditioned space
- **Best for**: Office buildings, retail, small commercial in moderate climates
- **Lifespan**: 15–20 years
- **Maintenance**: Similar to conventional HVAC — filter changes, coil cleaning

### Variable Refrigerant Flow (VRF) Systems
- **COP range**: 3.0–5.0
- **Cost**: $20–35 per square foot
- **Best for**: Multi-zone buildings needing simultaneous heating/cooling (offices, hotels)
- **Advantage**: Individual zone control, heat recovery between zones
- **Lifespan**: 15–25 years

### Ground-Source (Geothermal) Heat Pumps
- **COP range**: 4.0–6.0 (highest efficiency)
- **Cost**: $30–50 per square foot (includes bore field)
- **Best for**: Large campuses, new construction, buildings with available land
- **Advantage**: Constant source temperature — unaffected by outdoor conditions
- **Lifespan**: 25+ years (50+ for ground loop)
- **Limitation**: High upfront cost, land/drilling requirements

## Energy Savings
- Typical commercial building switching from gas boiler to heat pump:
  - **Heating energy reduction**: 40–60%
  - **Total energy cost reduction**: 20–35%
  - **Carbon emissions reduction**: 50–80% (depending on grid carbon intensity)

## Financial Incentives
- **IRA Section 179D**: Up to $5/sq ft deduction for qualifying efficiency improvements
- **IRA Section 48**: 30% ITC for geothermal heat pump systems
- **Utility rebates**: Varies by region — $1,000–$10,000 per system
- **C-PACE financing**: Covers 100% of project cost, repaid through property tax

## Climate Zone Considerations
| Climate Zone | Recommended Type | Notes |
|-------------|-----------------|-------|
| 1–2 (Hot) | ASHP or VRF | Primarily cooling; heating is supplemental |
| 3–4 (Mixed) | VRF or ASHP | Dual-mode operation critical |
| 5–6 (Cold) | ccASHP or VRF | Must be cold-climate rated |
| 7+ (Very Cold) | Ground-source | ASHP efficiency drops significantly |
