# Washington DC — Building Energy Performance Standards (BEPS)

## Overview
Washington DC's Building Energy Performance Standards (BEPS) are part of the Clean Energy DC Omnibus Act of 2018. DC was the first major U.S. city to establish building performance standards, requiring privately-owned buildings over 10,000 square feet to meet energy performance targets. The program is administered by the DC Department of Energy & Environment (DOEE).

## Applicability
- **Covered buildings**: Privately-owned buildings ≥ 10,000 gross square feet.
- **Municipal buildings**: DC government buildings ≥ 10,000 sq ft covered under separate requirements.
- **Estimated coverage**: Over 4,000 buildings across the District.
- **Building types**: Office, multifamily, retail, hotel, healthcare, education.

## Compliance Timeline
DC uses 5-year compliance cycles:
- **Cycle 1 (2021–2026)**: Buildings must meet ENERGY STAR score thresholds or demonstrate 20% energy reduction.
- **Cycle 2 (2027–2032)**: Stricter targets aligned with DC's goal of carbon neutrality by 2045.
- **Cycle 3 (2033–2038)**: Further tightening toward net-zero operations.

## Performance Targets
Targets are based on ENERGY STAR score or equivalent EUI reduction:
- **Minimum ENERGY STAR score**: 
  - Cycle 1: Score ≥ median for property type (typically 50)
  - Cycle 2: Score ≥ 60 (projected)
  - Cycle 3: Score ≥ 75 (projected)
- **Alternative pathway**: Demonstrate 20% EUI reduction from baseline per cycle.
- **Buildings without ENERGY STAR eligibility**: Must meet source EUI targets set by DOEE.

## Penalties
- **Non-compliance fine**: Up to $10 per square foot per year.
- **Reporting failures**: Separate penalties for failure to benchmark via ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager.
- **Alternative Compliance Pathway (ACP)**: Buildings unable to meet targets can pay into an alternative compliance fund or develop an approved compliance plan.
- **Example**: A 200,000 sq ft office failing to meet targets: up to **$2,000,000 annual penalty**.

## Mitigation Strategies
1. **Electrification**: Replace gas-fired boilers with air-source or ground-source heat pumps. DC's humid subtropical climate is well-suited for heat pump technology.
2. **Building retro-commissioning**: Optimize existing HVAC systems, fix simultaneous heating/cooling, repair economizers.
3. **LED lighting with controls**: High-efficiency lighting with daylight harvesting and occupancy sensors.
4. **Renewable energy procurement**: DC's Renewable Portfolio Standard requires 100% clean energy by 2032, benefiting buildings' grid emissions.
5. **Building envelope**: Window replacements, roof insulation, and air sealing for DC's variable climate.
6. **Smart building analytics**: Deploy fault detection and diagnostics (FDD) software to identify operational waste.

## DC-Specific Programs and Incentives
- **DC Sustainable Energy Utility (DCSEU)**: Provides rebates for energy efficiency and renewable energy projects — up to $200,000 per project for commercial buildings.
- **DC Green Building Act**: New construction and major renovations must meet green building standards.
- **DC Green Bank**: Low-interest financing for clean energy and energy efficiency projects.
- **Pepco incentives**: Utility-sponsored rebates for lighting, HVAC, and building controls.

## Key Metrics for Assessment
- Current ENERGY STAR score and trajectory toward target
- Current EUI vs. property type median EUI
- Fuel mix (natural gas vs. electric) — gas elimination is the fastest path to compliance
- DC grid emission factor: ~0.30 kgCO2/kWh (improving as RPS increases clean energy share)
