# FEMA Hazard Mitigation Grants

## Overview
FEMA offers several grant programs to fund pre-disaster and post-disaster mitigation projects for commercial and residential properties. These programs can cover 75–100% of eligible mitigation costs.

## Hazard Mitigation Grant Program (HMGP)
- **When**: Available after a presidential disaster declaration
- **Cost share**: 75% federal / 25% non-federal
- **Eligible activities**: Elevation, acquisition/demolition, wind retrofit, flood-proofing, utility protection, wildfire mitigation
- **Typical awards**: $50,000–$3,000,000 per project
- **Application**: Through state/local emergency management agencies
- **Timeline**: Applications typically due 12 months after disaster declaration

## Building Resilient Infrastructure and Communities (BRIC)
- **When**: Annual competitive grant program (pre-disaster)
- **Cost share**: 75% federal / 25% non-federal (up to 90% for small/impoverished communities)
- **Maximum award**: $50,000,000 per project
- **Priority areas**: Community lifelines, future-conditions risk assessment, nature-based solutions
- **2024 funding**: $1 billion available nationally
- **Application cycle**: Annual — typically opens in September

## Flood Mitigation Assistance (FMA)
- **Focus**: Specifically for NFIP-insured properties
- **Cost share**: Up to 100% for Severe Repetitive Loss properties; 75% for other NFIP properties
- **Eligible activities**: Property elevation, acquisition, mitigation reconstruction, dry/wet flood-proofing
- **Priority**: Repetitive Loss and Severe Repetitive Loss properties

## Increased Cost of Compliance (ICC)
- **Available**: As part of standard NFIP flood insurance policy
- **Maximum benefit**: $30,000 per claim
- **Trigger**: Building declared substantially damaged or repetitive loss
- **Eligible uses**: Elevation, flood-proofing, demolition, relocation
- **Key point**: This is insurance benefit, not a grant — available to all NFIP policyholders

## Application Tips for Commercial Properties
1. Obtain an elevation certificate from a licensed surveyor
2. Document all historical flood damage with photos, repair invoices, insurance claims
3. Get a Benefit-Cost Analysis (BCA) — FEMA requires BCR ≥ 1.0
4. Work with your state's hazard mitigation office early — they manage the application process
5. Consider bundling multiple properties for a single community-level application
