# GRESB — Global Real Estate Sustainability Benchmark

## Overview
GRESB (Global Real Estate Sustainability Benchmark) is the leading ESG benchmark for real estate and infrastructure. Over 2,000 real estate entities and funds participate globally, covering $8.6+ trillion in assets under management. GRESB scores are used by 170+ institutional investors (pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, asset managers) to evaluate and compare ESG performance of real estate investments.

## Scoring Structure
GRESB scores range from 0 to 100, based on two dimensions:

### Management & Policy (30% of total score)
- **Leadership**: Sustainability strategy, ESG targets, board-level oversight
- **Policies**: Environmental and social policies, health & safety, stakeholder engagement
- **Reporting**: Disclosure practices, third-party reporting (CDP, TCFD, SASB)
- **Risk Management**: Climate risk assessment, resilience planning, supply chain

### Implementation & Measurement (70% of total score)
- **Energy**: Energy consumption data, intensity metrics, reduction programs
- **GHG Emissions**: Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions data, reduction targets, verification
- **Water**: Water consumption data and conservation measures
- **Waste**: Waste management data, diversion rates, circularity
- **Building Certifications**: LEED, ENERGY STAR, BREEAM, WELL certifications
- **Tenant Engagement**: Green lease provisions, sustainability communications

## GRESB Rating System (5 Stars)
| Rating | Quintile | Approximate Score Range |
|--------|----------|------------------------|
| ★★★★★ | Top 20% | 85–100 |
| ★★★★ | 21–40% | 70–84 |
| ★★★ | 41–60% | 55–69 |
| ★★ | 61–80% | 40–54 |
| ★ | Bottom 20% | 0–39 |

## How Sustainability Retrofits Improve GRESB Scores

### Energy Efficiency Measures
- **LED lighting**: Improves Energy indicator through reduced consumption and measurable savings data.
- **BMS upgrades**: Demonstrates energy management system implementation (scored indicator).
- **HVAC upgrades (heat pumps)**: Reduces energy intensity and GHG emissions — impacts both Energy and Emissions indicators.
- **Building envelope**: Measurable EUI reduction contributes to energy performance trending.

### Certifications (High-Impact Scoring)
- **ENERGY STAR certification**: Directly scored — certified buildings receive full points for the building certification indicator.
- **LEED certification**: Higher tiers (Gold, Platinum) provide maximum points.
- **WELL certification**: Increasingly valued; contributes to Health & Wellbeing indicators.

### Resilience and Risk Management
- **Climate risk assessment**: Documenting physical and transition risk analysis earns points in Risk Management.
- **CRREM alignment**: Demonstrating Paris-aligned pathways strengthens the climate risk narrative.
- **Flood/wildfire mitigation**: Physical resilience measures documented as risk management.

### Green Leases
- **Green lease clauses**: Data sharing, energy targets, fit-out standards with tenants — high-value indicator.
- **Tenant engagement programs**: Sustainability guides, energy challenges, green building training.

## 2025 GRESB Changes
- **New**: Scored recognition for operationally efficient assets (high Energy Star scores rewarded independently of year-over-year improvement).
- **Data collection (unscored)**: Renewable energy procurement details, embodied carbon, biodiversity impact.
- **Sector-specific**: Enhanced residential-specific indicators and weights.
- **Retired**: Personnel responsible for ESG and Employee Engagement indicators simplified.

## Strategic Value for JLL Clients
- **Investor relations**: GRESB scores directly influence institutional investor allocation decisions. A 1-star improvement can materially impact capital access.
- **Competitive positioning**: GRESB peer benchmarks show how a fund/portfolio compares to competitors.
- **ESG integration**: GRESB provides a structured framework to demonstrate ESG commitment to stakeholders.
- **Premium pricing**: Research shows GRESB 5-star assets command 3–8% rent premiums and 5–15% valuation premiums.

## Key Metrics for Assessment
- Current GRESB score and star rating
- Year-over-year energy consumption trends (absolute and intensity)
- Percentage of portfolio with building certifications (LEED, ENERGY STAR)
- Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions data coverage
- Green lease adoption rate across portfolio
- Climate risk assessment coverage (% of assets assessed)
