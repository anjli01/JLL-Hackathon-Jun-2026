# Green Premium & Brown Discount — Real Estate Market Impact

## Overview
Research by JLL, CBRE, MSCI, and academic institutions consistently demonstrates that sustainability-certified and climate-resilient buildings command higher valuations ("green premium"), while poorly performing buildings face discounts ("brown discount"). This trend is accelerating as ESG mandates, tenant preferences, and regulatory requirements tighten.

## Green Premium Evidence
### Rental Premium
- **LEED-certified offices**: 3–8% rental premium (JLL Research, 2024)
- **ENERGY STAR certified**: 3–5% rental premium
- **WELL-certified**: 4–7% rental premium (particularly post-COVID)
- **Combined certifications**: Up to 12% premium for LEED Platinum + WELL Gold

### Occupancy Rates
- **Green-certified buildings**: 4–6% higher occupancy (CBRE, 2023)
- **In tight markets**: Premium narrows; in soft markets, premium widens significantly
- **Tenant retention**: 15–20% higher retention rates in certified buildings

### Transaction Value
- **Sale price premium**: 10–25% for green-certified buildings (MSCI/Real Capital Analytics)
- **Cap rate compression**: 20–50 bps lower cap rates for certified assets
- **Investor preference**: 78% of institutional investors consider ESG in investment decisions (PRI, 2024)

## Brown Discount Evidence
### Stranded Asset Risk
- Buildings that cannot meet tightening emissions standards face:
  - **Capital expenditure cliff**: Deferred maintenance + required retrofits = major capital calls
  - **Regulatory penalties**: LL97 penalties of $268/tCO2, BERDO payments of $234/tCO2
  - **Insurance cost increases**: 15–30% higher premiums for high-risk, non-mitigated buildings
  - **Financing restrictions**: Green loan requirements increasingly exclude brown buildings

### Market Evidence
- **Non-certified offices**: 5–10% rental discount vs. certified peers (JLL, 2024)
- **High-EUI buildings**: 8–15% lower transaction values
- **Climate-exposed assets**: 5–20% discount in flood/heat/wildfire zones without mitigation

## Strategic Implications for JLL Advisory
1. **For acquisitions**: Climate risk scoring identifies brown discount opportunities for value-add investors
2. **For dispositions**: Sustainability certification maximizes exit value
3. **For leasing**: Green credentials attract premium tenants and reduce vacancy
4. **For asset management**: Proactive mitigation preserves value and avoids stranded asset risk
5. **For client advisory**: Quantified green premium/brown discount data strengthens the business case for sustainability investments
