# Seattle Building Emissions Performance Standard (BEPS)

## Overview
Seattle's Building Emissions Performance Standard (BEPS), signed into law in December 2023, establishes greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions intensity targets for nonresidential and multifamily buildings over 20,000 square feet. It is one of the most aggressive building performance standards in the United States, with penalties reaching up to $10 per square foot for non-compliance.

## Applicability
- **Covered buildings**: Nonresidential and multifamily buildings ≥ 20,000 gross square feet.
- **Exemptions**: Buildings with Energy Star scores ≥ 90, recent major renovations meeting current energy code, certain affordable housing properties, and buildings scheduled for demolition.
- **Estimated coverage**: Approximately 3,500 buildings in Seattle.

## Compliance Timeline
- **October 1, 2027**: First benchmarking and GHG reporting deadline for buildings ≥ 220,000 sq ft.
- **2028**: Reporting extends to buildings ≥ 100,000 sq ft.
- **2029**: Reporting extends to buildings ≥ 50,000 sq ft.
- **2030**: Reporting extends to all covered buildings (≥ 20,000 sq ft).
- **2031–2035**: First compliance period — buildings must meet initial GHG intensity targets.
- **2036–2040**: Second compliance period — stricter targets.

## GHG Intensity Targets
Targets are set as greenhouse gas intensity (GHGI) measured in kgCO2e per square foot per year, varying by building type:

| Building Type | 2031 Target (kgCO2e/sq ft/yr) | 2036 Target |
|---------------|-------------------------------|-------------|
| Office | ~3.5 | ~2.0 |
| Multifamily | ~4.0 | ~2.5 |
| Retail | ~4.5 | ~2.5 |
| Hotel | ~5.0 | ~3.0 |

## Penalties
- **Failure to report / inaccurate reports**:
  - $4,000/year for buildings ≥ 50,000 sq ft
  - $2,000/year for buildings 20,000–49,999 sq ft
- **Failure to meet GHG intensity targets (starting 2031)**:
  - **$2.50/sq ft** for low-income/low-rent housing
  - **$7.50/sq ft** for other multifamily buildings
  - **$10.00/sq ft** for nonresidential buildings
- **Example**: A 100,000 sq ft office building failing to meet targets: **$1,000,000 annual penalty**.

## Mitigation Strategies
1. **Electrification**: Replace natural gas heating with heat pumps. Seattle's grid is ~90% hydroelectric, making electrification highly effective for emissions reduction.
2. **Building envelope upgrades**: Insulation, window replacement, air sealing to reduce heating demand.
3. **LED lighting and controls**: High-efficiency lighting with occupancy sensors and daylight harvesting.
4. **On-site solar PV**: Reduces grid electricity consumption and associated emissions.
5. **Building tune-ups**: Seattle's existing Building Tune-Up program requires periodic operational optimization.
6. **Energy management systems**: Smart BMS for HVAC scheduling, demand response, and monitoring.

## Seattle City Light Clean Energy
Seattle benefits from one of the cleanest electricity grids in the U.S. (~90% hydroelectric). This means electrification of heating is particularly effective — switching from gas to electric heat pumps can reduce building emissions by 80–90%.

## Key Metrics for Assessment
- Current GHGI vs. target GHGI for building type
- Natural gas consumption (primary driver of on-site emissions)
- Seattle City Light grid emission factor: ~0.03 kgCO2/kWh (very low due to hydro)
- Natural gas emission factor: 5.3 kgCO2/therm
