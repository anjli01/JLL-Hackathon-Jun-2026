# IRA Tax Credits — Inflation Reduction Act of 2022

## Overview
The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) is the largest climate investment in U.S. history, providing over $369 billion in energy and climate-related tax credits, grants, and incentives. Commercial real estate benefits from several key provisions.

## Section 179D — Commercial Building Energy Efficiency Deduction
- **Eligible**: Commercial buildings that achieve energy savings of at least 25% relative to ASHRAE 90.1-2007 reference building.
- **Base deduction**: $0.50–$1.00 per square foot (based on energy savings percentage).
- **Bonus deduction**: Up to **$5.00 per square foot** if prevailing wage and apprenticeship requirements are met.
- **Eligible measures**: HVAC, lighting, building envelope improvements, and whole-building energy reductions.
- **Example**: A 200,000 sq ft office achieving 40% energy savings with prevailing wage: **$1,000,000 deduction**.
- **Stackable**: Can be claimed alongside state/local incentives and utility rebates.

## Section 48 — Investment Tax Credit (ITC) for Solar and Energy Storage
- **Solar PV**: **30% ITC** for commercial solar installations through 2032. Reduces to 26% in 2033, 22% in 2034.
- **Battery storage**: **30% ITC** for standalone energy storage (new under IRA — previously required solar pairing).
- **Bonus adders**:
  - +10% for domestic content (US-manufactured components)
  - +10% for projects in energy communities (coal/fossil fuel transition areas)
  - +10–20% for projects in low-income communities
- **Example**: A $500,000 rooftop solar installation: **$150,000 tax credit** (30%).

## Section 45L — New Energy Efficient Home Credit
- **Eligible**: New residential construction meeting ENERGY STAR or Zero Energy Ready Home standards.
- **Single-family**: $2,500 (ENERGY STAR) to $5,000 (Zero Energy Ready) per unit.
- **Multifamily**: $500 to $5,000 per unit depending on certification level and prevailing wage compliance.

## Section 48E — Clean Electricity Investment Tax Credit (post-2025)
- Technology-neutral ITC for clean electricity generation starting 2025.
- **30% credit** for qualifying clean energy projects.
- Replaces technology-specific credits with emissions-based qualification.

## Direct Pay and Transferability
- **Direct pay (elective pay)**: Tax-exempt entities (nonprofits, government, tribal) can receive credits as direct payments.
- **Transferability**: For-profit entities can sell tax credits to unrelated third parties for cash.
- This dramatically expands the market for clean energy financing.

## PACE Financing (Property Assessed Clean Energy)
- While not an IRA provision, PACE financing complements IRA credits:
  - **C-PACE**: Commercial PACE covers 100% of eligible project costs.
  - Repaid through property tax assessment over 15–25 years.
  - Non-recourse: stays with the property, not the owner.
  - Available in 37+ states.
  - Covers: solar, HVAC, lighting, envelope, water conservation, resilience measures.
