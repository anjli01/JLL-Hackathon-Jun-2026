# BERDO 2.0 — Boston Building Emissions Reduction and Disclosure Ordinance

## Overview
BERDO 2.0, enacted in 2021, requires large existing buildings in Boston to achieve net-zero carbon emissions by 2050. It covers approximately 3,500 buildings over 20,000 square feet and is one of the most ambitious building performance standards in the United States.

## Compliance Timeline
- **2025**: First emissions reporting deadline.
- **2030**: Buildings must meet initial emissions reduction targets (approximately 50% below 2005 baseline).
- **2035**: Stricter interim targets.
- **2040**: Further tightening.
- **2050**: Net-zero carbon emissions required for all covered buildings.

## Emissions Standards
- Standards are set based on building type and are expressed in kg CO2e per square foot per year.
- Buildings can comply through direct emissions reductions, renewable energy procurement, or alternative compliance payments.

## Penalties
- **Alternative compliance payments** of approximately $234 per metric ton of CO2 over the limit.
- Buildings that fail to report face fines of up to $1,000 per day.
- Repeat violations may result in liens on the property.

## Mitigation Strategies
1. **Electrification of heating**: Boston's cold climate makes heat pump adoption critical. Cold-climate air-source heat pumps (ccASHP) operate efficiently down to -15°F.
2. **Building envelope improvements**: Critical for Boston's heating-dominated climate — insulation, triple-pane windows, air sealing.
3. **Renewable energy procurement**: Purchase RECs or enter PPAs for off-site wind/solar.
4. **District energy systems**: Boston has expanding district heating/cooling networks.
5. **Demand response programs**: Participate in ISO-NE demand response for grid flexibility and revenue.

## MassSave Incentives
Massachusetts' MassSave program offers significant incentives:
- **Heat pump rebates**: Up to $10,000 per system for commercial cold-climate heat pumps.
- **Insulation subsidies**: 75–100% coverage for attic and wall insulation.
- **Lighting incentives**: $0.50–$2.00 per watt for LED conversions.
- **Custom incentives**: Engineering studies and custom rebates for complex projects.
- **0% financing**: Available for qualifying energy efficiency projects.
