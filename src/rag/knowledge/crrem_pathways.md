# CRREM — Carbon Risk Real Estate Monitor

## Overview
The Carbon Risk Real Estate Monitor (CRREM) is a science-based framework that provides decarbonization pathways for the global real estate sector. It translates the Paris Agreement's 1.5°C and 2°C warming targets into property-type-specific and country-specific carbon and energy intensity trajectories through 2050. CRREM is the standard used by institutional investors, GRESB, and TCFD-aligned reporting to assess transition risk and stranding potential.

## What is Stranding Risk?
A building becomes "stranded" when its carbon or energy intensity exceeds the CRREM decarbonization pathway. At this point, the asset:
- Faces regulatory penalties and compliance costs
- May lose access to green financing (green bonds, sustainability-linked loans)
- Risks depreciation relative to Paris-aligned peers
- May become difficult to lease to ESG-conscious tenants
- Insurance premiums may increase due to transition risk perception

The **"Stranding Year"** (or "Misalignment Year") is when a building's projected emissions trajectory crosses above the CRREM pathway — a critical metric for investment decisions.

## How to Use CRREM for Portfolio Assessment
1. **Calculate current intensity**: Determine the building's energy use intensity (EUI) and carbon intensity (GHG/m²/yr) using actual utility data.
2. **Compare to pathway**: Plot current intensity against the CRREM pathway for the building type and country.
3. **Identify stranding year**: Project future intensity (factoring in grid decarbonization, planned retrofits, etc.) and find where it crosses the pathway.
4. **Prioritize actions**: Buildings with near-term stranding years (before 2030) should be prioritized for retrofit investment.

## Key Levers to Align with CRREM Pathways
1. **Grid decarbonization**: As utility grids add renewable capacity, building emissions naturally decline — but this alone is rarely sufficient.
2. **Energy efficiency**: Reduce EUI through envelope upgrades, HVAC optimization, LED lighting, and BMS controls. Typically achieves 20–40% reduction.
3. **Electrification**: Replace fossil fuel systems (gas boilers, steam) with heat pumps. Eliminates Scope 1 on-site emissions.
4. **On-site renewables**: Solar PV and battery storage reduce grid electricity consumption and associated Scope 2 emissions.
5. **Renewable energy procurement**: Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) or Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs) to reduce Scope 2.

## CRREM and GRESB
GRESB uses CRREM pathways as the reference standard for assessing portfolio-level decarbonization alignment. Buildings aligned with CRREM targets score higher on GRESB's Implementation & Measurement dimension, particularly in:
- Energy consumption data quality
- GHG emissions reduction targets
- Building certification alignment
- Climate risk assessment documentation

## CRREM and Financial Risk
- **Green bond eligibility**: Many green bond frameworks require CRREM alignment for real estate assets.
- **Sustainability-linked loans**: Interest rate margins may be tied to CRREM pathway compliance.
- **Valuation impact**: Research indicates a 5–15% valuation premium for Paris-aligned assets versus stranded assets.
- **Due diligence**: Institutional investors increasingly require CRREM analysis in acquisition due diligence.
