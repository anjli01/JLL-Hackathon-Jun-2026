# California Climate Regulations — SB 253, SB 261, and Building Standards

## Overview
California leads the U.S. in climate-related building regulations with a combination of mandatory GHG emissions disclosure (SB 253), climate risk reporting (SB 261), all-electric new construction mandates (Title 24/CalGreen), and local building performance standards in cities like Los Angeles and San Francisco.

## SB 253 — Climate Corporate Data Accountability Act (2024)
- **Requirement**: Companies with annual revenues exceeding $1 billion that do business in California must report Scope 1, 2, and 3 greenhouse gas emissions.
- **Timeline**: Scope 1 & 2 reporting begins 2026; Scope 3 reporting begins 2027.
- **Relevance to CRE**: Real estate companies, REITs, and property management firms meeting the revenue threshold must disclose building-level emissions data. This drives demand for energy audits, sub-metering, and emissions tracking.
- **Assurance requirement**: Third-party limited assurance for Scope 1 & 2 required starting 2026; reasonable assurance starting 2030.

## SB 261 — Climate-Related Financial Risk Act (2024)
- **Requirement**: Companies with revenues exceeding $500 million must prepare and publish climate-related financial risk reports aligned with TCFD framework.
- **Timeline**: First reports due by January 1, 2026, and biennially thereafter.
- **CRE impact**: Real estate companies must disclose physical climate risks (flood, wildfire, heat) and transition risks (regulatory penalties, stranded assets) across their portfolios.
- **Report contents**: Governance, strategy, risk management, metrics and targets aligned with TCFD.

## Title 24 — California Building Energy Efficiency Standards
- **2022 code cycle (effective Jan 2023)**:
  - All-electric baseline for new construction (heat pump HVAC and water heating required).
  - Solar PV required on new commercial buildings (certain sizes).
  - Battery storage encouraged (solar + storage requirement for some building types).
- **2025 code cycle (effective Jan 2026)**:
  - Stricter envelope requirements.
  - Expanded electric-ready requirements for existing building renovations.
  - Enhanced grid-interactive capabilities.

## Los Angeles — Existing Buildings Energy and Water Efficiency (EBEWE) Program
- **Covered buildings**: Commercial buildings ≥ 20,000 sq ft.
- **Requirements**: Annual benchmarking, energy/water audits every 5 years, retro-commissioning.
- **Penalties**: Up to $201 per day for non-compliance with benchmarking.
- **AB 802**: California state law requiring ENERGY STAR benchmarking for commercial buildings ≥ 50,000 sq ft.

## San Francisco — Building Emission Performance Ordinance
- **Covered buildings**: Commercial and multifamily buildings ≥ 25,000 sq ft.
- **Requirements**: Meet emissions performance targets or demonstrate compliance pathway.
- **Timeline**: Targets phase in 2025–2030.

## California-Specific Incentives
- **SGIP (Self-Generation Incentive Program)**: Up to $1.00/Wh for battery storage, especially in fire-threat areas (enhanced incentives).
- **CalSHAPE**: School and public building energy efficiency grants.
- **Title 24 compliance incentives**: Utility rebates through PG&E, SCE, and SDG&E for exceeding code requirements.
- **California ITC adder**: Federal IRA provides +10% ITC bonus for projects in energy communities, many of which are in California's Central Valley and former fossil fuel areas.
- **GoGreen Financing**: State-supported PACE and on-bill financing programs.

## Key Metrics for Assessment
- Current EUI vs. Title 24 targets
- GHG emissions per sq ft (for SB 253 reporting)
- Climate risk exposure score (for SB 261 reporting)
- ENERGY STAR score and benchmarking compliance status
- Grid emission factor: PG&E ~0.10 kgCO2/kWh, SCE ~0.15 kgCO2/kWh (relatively clean due to California RPS)
