# Cool Roof Technology — Heat Mitigation for Commercial Buildings

## Overview
Cool roofs reflect more sunlight and absorb less heat than standard roofs, reducing roof surface temperatures by up to 50–60°F. They are one of the most cost-effective interventions for buildings with high heat risk and cooling loads.

## Types
### Reflective Coatings
- Applied over existing roof membranes (TPO, EPDM, modified bitumen)
- Solar Reflectance Index (SRI) ≥ 78
- Cost: $3–5 per square foot (retrofit)
- Lifespan extension: 5–10 years for existing roof

### Single-Ply Membranes (TPO/PVC)
- Factory-applied reflective surface
- SRI ≥ 82
- Cost: $8–12 per square foot (full re-roof)
- Lifespan: 20–30 years

### Green Roofs (Vegetated)
- Extensive (sedum, 2–6 inches soil): $15–25/sq ft
- Intensive (garden, 6+ inches soil): $25–50/sq ft
- Additional benefits: stormwater management, urban heat island reduction, biodiversity
- Maintenance required: irrigation, weeding, seasonal care

## Energy Savings
- **Cooling energy reduction**: 10–30% (greatest impact in hot climates)
- **Peak demand reduction**: 10–15% (reduces HVAC sizing requirements)
- **Annual cost savings**: $0.50–$0.80 per square foot
- **Payback period**: 5–7 years for coatings; 8–12 years for full re-roof

## Climate Suitability
| Climate Zone | Effectiveness | Recommendation |
|-------------|--------------|----------------|
| 1–2 (Hot-humid/dry) | High | Strongly recommended — largest cooling savings |
| 3 (Warm) | High | Recommended — good ROI |
| 4 (Mixed) | Moderate | Recommended if cooling-dominant |
| 5+ (Cold) | Low-Moderate | May increase heating costs in winter — evaluate net impact |

## Incentives
- Many utility companies offer $0.10–$0.30 per sq ft rebates for cool roof installations
- Qualifies as part of IRA Section 179D building envelope improvements
- Can contribute to LEED credits (Sustainable Sites — Heat Island Reduction, 1–2 points)

## Heat Risk Mitigation
- For properties flagged with high heat risk scores:
  - Prioritize cool roofs as a quick-win intervention
  - Combine with exterior shading (awnings, trees) for maximum impact
  - Consider reflective wall coatings for east/west facades
  - Install rooftop solar panels (provides shading + renewable energy)
