# LED Lighting — Commercial Building Efficiency

## Overview
LED lighting retrofits are the single most cost-effective energy efficiency measure for commercial buildings, offering 40–60% electricity savings on lighting with payback periods of 2–4 years.

## Technology Specifications
- **Efficacy**: 100–200 lumens per watt (vs. 15–20 for incandescent, 50–80 for fluorescent)
- **Lifespan**: 50,000–100,000 hours (10–25 years at typical commercial usage)
- **Color Rendering Index (CRI)**: 80–95+ (excellent for office and retail environments)
- **Dimming**: Compatible with 0–10V, DALI, and wireless dimming protocols
- **Instant-on**: No warm-up time (unlike fluorescent and HID)

## Cost and Savings

| Exterior (wall pack) | $80–150 | $40–70 | 2–3 years |
| High bay (warehouse) | $150–300 | $80–150 | 2–3 years |

## Advanced Controls
- **Occupancy sensors**: Additional 20–30% savings beyond LED conversion
- **Daylight harvesting**: Dims lights when natural light is sufficient — 10–20% additional savings
- **Networked lighting controls (NLC)**: Enables scheduling, zone control, demand response
- **IoT integration**: Sensors embedded in fixtures for space utilization analytics

## Financial Incentives
- **Utility rebates**: Most utilities offer $0.50–$2.00 per watt for LED conversions
- **IRA Section 179D**: LED retrofits count toward the building energy efficiency deduction
- **LEED credits**: Contributes to Energy & Atmosphere optimization credits
- **Accelerated depreciation**: LED fixtures qualify for bonus depreciation under IRS rules

## Implementation Strategy
1. **Phase 1 — Quick wins**: Replace highest-use fixtures first (24/7 areas, lobbies, parking)
2. **Phase 2 — Office spaces**: Retrofit office troffers with LED panels + occupancy sensors
3. **Phase 3 — Advanced controls**: Deploy networked lighting controls for demand response capability
