# Energize Denver — Building Performance Standard

## Overview
Energize Denver is Denver's building energy performance ordinance, applying to commercial and multifamily buildings 25,000 square feet and larger. It sets Energy Use Intensity (EUI) targets by building type and requires annual benchmarking. Denver also has state-level overlap with Building Performance Colorado (BPC).

## Applicability
- **Covered buildings**: Commercial and multifamily buildings ≥ 25,000 gross square feet.
- **Estimated coverage**: Approximately 1,500 buildings in Denver.
- **State overlap**: Buildings ≥ 50,000 sq ft may also be subject to Building Performance Colorado (BPC). Energize Denver currently provides "deemed compliance" for state targets, but buildings must benchmark for both.

## Compliance Timeline
- **Annual benchmarking**: Due by June 1 each year via ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager.
- **2027**: Interim EUI performance targets take effect.
- **2026**: Third-party data verification required.
- **2030**: Stricter interim EUI targets.
- **2032**: Final performance targets.

## EUI Targets (kBtu/sq ft/year)
Targets vary by building type and become more stringent over time:

| Building Type | 2027 Target | 2030 Target | 2032 Target |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Office | 75 | 60 | 50 |
| Multifamily | 65 | 55 | 45 |
| Retail | 80 | 65 | 55 |
| Hotel/Lodging | 95 | 80 | 65 |
| K-12 School | 55 | 45 | 38 |
| Healthcare | 140 | 120 | 100 |

## Penalties
- **Non-compliance with EUI targets**: $0.30–$0.70 per kBtu over the building's target, assessed annually.
- **Failure to benchmark**: Fines for non-reporting.
- **State-level (BPC)**: Additional monthly penalties for buildings ≥ 50,000 sq ft that fail state performance targets.
- **Example**: A 100,000 sq ft office with EUI of 90 kBtu/sq ft (target 75): overage = 15 kBtu × 100,000 sq ft × $0.50/kBtu = **$750,000 annual penalty**.

## Mitigation Strategies
1. **Building tune-ups and retro-commissioning**: Often achieves 10–20% EUI reduction through operational improvements — fixing scheduling drift, demand spikes, and simultaneous heating/cooling.
2. **HVAC upgrades**: Replace aging boilers and chillers with high-efficiency heat pumps. Denver's climate (cold winters, hot summers) makes dual-fuel or cold-climate heat pumps ideal.
3. **LED lighting retrofits**: Reduces lighting energy by 40–60% and decreases cooling loads.
4. **Building envelope improvements**: Insulation and window upgrades critical for Denver's 5,280 ft elevation and high solar gain.
5. **Smart building controls (BMS)**: Automated scheduling, demand-controlled ventilation, economizer optimization.
6. **Solar PV**: Denver averages 300+ sunny days per year — excellent solar resource for on-site generation.

## Denver-Specific Incentives
- **Xcel Energy rebates**: Commercial lighting, HVAC, and building envelope rebates through Xcel Energy's efficiency programs.
- **Denver CLEER (Climate, Legislation, Energy, Equity, Resilience)**: City program providing technical assistance and resources for compliance.
- **Colorado C-PACE**: Commercial Property Assessed Clean Energy financing available statewide.

## Key Metrics for Assessment
- Current EUI vs. target EUI for building type and compliance year
- Natural gas vs. electricity split (gas reduction is primary lever in Denver)
- ENERGY STAR score (buildings scoring ≥ 75 are likely compliant)
- Denver's grid emission factor: ~0.55 kgCO2/kWh (Xcel Energy mix)
