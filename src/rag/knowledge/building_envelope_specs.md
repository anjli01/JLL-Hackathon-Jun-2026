# Building Envelope Upgrades — Insulation, Windows, and Air Sealing

## Overview
Building envelope upgrades — insulation, windows, and air sealing — are foundational energy efficiency measures for commercial buildings. The envelope is responsible for 25–40% of total heating and cooling loads. Improving it reduces HVAC sizing requirements, lowers energy costs, improves occupant comfort, and extends equipment life. Envelope upgrades are prerequisites for right-sizing heat pump installations during electrification.

## Window Specifications

### Performance Metrics
- **U-factor**: Measures heat transfer rate. Lower is better. Target ≤ 0.30 BTU/hr·ft²·°F for commercial.
- **SHGC (Solar Heat Gain Coefficient)**: Measures solar heat transmission. Lower values reduce cooling loads in hot climates.
- **VLT (Visible Light Transmittance)**: Higher values mean more natural light — reduces lighting energy.
- **Air Leakage**: Target ≤ 0.30 cfm/ft² per ASHRAE 90.1.

## Air Sealing
Air infiltration accounts for 25–40% of heating/cooling energy loss in commercial buildings.

### Key Sealing Points
- Window and door perimeters
- Curtain wall connections
- Roof penetrations (mechanical, plumbing, electrical)
- Loading dock doors and vestibules
- Floor-to-wall joints
- Elevator shafts and stairwells (stack effect)

### Testing and Targets
- **Blower door testing**: Measures air changes per hour (ACH50). Target ≤ 3.0 ACH50 for commercial buildings.
- **Thermal imaging**: Identifies heat loss and air leakage points.
- **Typical savings**: Air sealing alone can reduce heating/cooling energy by 10–25%.

## ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Compliance
The latest ASHRAE 90.1-2022 standard requires:
- Continuous insulation on all commercial wall assemblies (eliminates thermal bridging).
- Enhanced fenestration performance (lower U-factors and SHGC requirements).
- Mandatory air barrier requirements with testing for larger buildings.
- These standards are increasingly adopted as the baseline for building codes and IRA Section 179D qualification.

## Stacking with Other Measures
Building envelope upgrades are most effective when combined with:
1. **Heat pump installation**: Reduced thermal loads mean smaller, less expensive heat pump systems.
2. **LED lighting**: Combined with window improvements, reduces both lighting and cooling energy.
3. **Solar PV**: Lower energy consumption means a smaller solar array covers a larger percentage of remaining load.
4. **IRA Section 179D**: Envelope upgrades count toward the 25% energy savings threshold for the $0.50–$5.00/sq ft deduction.
