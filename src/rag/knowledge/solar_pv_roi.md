# Solar PV — Commercial Rooftop and On-Site Installations

## Overview
Commercial rooftop solar PV provides on-site renewable energy generation, reducing grid electricity costs and carbon emissions. The IRA's 30% Investment Tax Credit makes solar one of the highest-ROI investments in commercial real estate.

## System Sizing
- **Typical commercial roof**: 5–10 watts per square foot of available roof area
- **Office building (100,000 sq ft roof)**: 200–500 kW system, generating 250,000–650,000 kWh/year
- **Annual production**: 1,300–1,800 kWh per kW installed (varies by location and tilt)
- **Roof coverage**: Typically 60–80% of usable roof area (accounting for setbacks, equipment, shading)

## Cost and ROI
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Installed cost | $2.00–$3.50 per watt (commercial scale) |
| IRA ITC (30%) | Reduces net cost by 30% |
| LCOE | $0.04–$0.08 per kWh (after incentives) |
| Annual savings | $1.50–$2.50 per sq ft of panel area |
| Simple payback | 5–8 years (after tax credit) |
| System lifespan | 25–30 years |
| Performance degradation | 0.3–0.5% per year |

## Financial Structure Options
1. **Direct purchase**: Building owner buys system. Captures ITC, depreciation, and all energy savings.
2. **PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)**: Third party owns system, sells power to building at fixed rate (typically 10–20% below utility rate). No upfront cost.
3. **Lease**: Fixed monthly payment for solar system. Predictable costs, no ownership complexity.
4. **Community solar**: Subscribe to off-site solar farm. Receive credits on utility bill. No rooftop needed.

## Incentives (Beyond IRA ITC)
- **Net metering**: Many states require utilities to credit excess generation at retail rate.
- **SRECs**: Solar Renewable Energy Credits — additional revenue in states like NJ, MA, DC ($50–$300/MWh).
- **NY-Sun**: New York offers up to $0.20/watt for commercial solar.
- **Accelerated depreciation (MACRS)**: 5-year depreciation schedule for solar equipment.
- **State rebates**: Vary widely — California SGIP, Massachusetts SMART, etc.

## Integration with Building Performance
- Solar PV reduces grid electricity consumption → lowers LL97/BERDO emissions
- Pairs well with battery storage for demand charge management and resilience
- Can provide rooftop shading benefit (reduces cooling load by 2–5%)
- Contributes to LEED Renewable Energy credits (1–5 points)
