# Building Management System (BMS) Upgrades

## Overview
A modern BMS integrates HVAC, lighting, fire/life safety, and other building systems into a unified platform with real-time monitoring, automated controls, and data analytics. Upgrading from legacy pneumatic or standalone controls to a modern BMS is one of the highest-impact energy efficiency measures.

## Key Capabilities
- **HVAC optimization**: Automated scheduling, setpoint management, demand-controlled ventilation
- **Fault detection and diagnostics (FDD)**: Identifies equipment issues before they become costly failures
- **Demand response**: Automatically reduces load during grid peak events (earns revenue)
- **Energy monitoring**: Real-time dashboards, anomaly detection, benchmarking
- **Tenant comfort management**: Zone-level temperature control, complaint tracking

## Energy Impact
- **Total energy savings**: 10–25% of HVAC energy consumption
- **Peak demand reduction**: 15–20% through load shifting and optimization
- **Maintenance cost reduction**: 10–15% through predictive maintenance
- **Equipment life extension**: 15–25% longer life through optimized operation

## Integration with Compliance
- BMS data provides automated reporting for LL97, BERDO, and other BPS requirements
- Real-time emissions tracking against regulatory limits
- Automated alerts when building approaches compliance thresholds
- Documentation for LEED Energy & Atmosphere credits (Enhanced Commissioning)
